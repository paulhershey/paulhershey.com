MY APPROACH TO DESIGN THINKING

NO.1

Always start with the research.

The first step should always be discovery. Start by performing primary and secondary research. Empathize with the users and learn as much as you can about their needs and goals. Validate your questions through qualitative research.

Your old site is the prototype of the new site.

- Hoa Lorangers @ Nielsen Norman Group

NO.2

Define goals and success.

After working through a lot of unstructured research, it’s time to converge on the idea you want to develop. A few ways to approach this would be through competitive analysis, ideation sessions, empathy mapping, HMW statements, and KPI’s.

Design for spread and scale.

- Denise Gershbein @ Frog

NO.3

Ideate on possible solutions.

At this point it’s important to ideate over as many solutions as possible. Keeping an open mind and exploring radical ideas could shine light on powerful solutions. Get as many ideas out as possible and vote as group on best solutions.

Innovation is often a product of constraint.

- Todd Waterbury @ Target

NO.4

Prototype and improve ideas.

It’s important to test at every step of design and development. From paper prototypes to coded views, you should validate your ideas every step of the way ensuring you are always on the right path and creating features your users need and love.

Focus unswervingly on the customer.

- Jesse Hertzberg @ Squarespace

NO.5

Validate your findings.

Once features have been released into a product, it’s good to validate with data. Data is a tally of interacts and can inform decisions as well as validate KPI’s. It’s important to keep the user first and ensure you build something they want to use.

Netflix is unapologetically data driven.

- Rochelle King @ Netflix
