MY APPROACH TO DESIGN THINKING

NO.1

Always start with the research.

The first step should always be discovery, so I start with primary and secondary research. I empathize with users, learn as much as I can about their needs and goals, and validate my questions through qualitative research.

The old site is the prototype of the new site.

- Hoa Lorangers @ Nielsen Norman Group

NO.2

Define goals and KPI's.

After working through a lot of unstructured research, it’s time to converge on an idea. I approach this through competitive analysis, ideation sessions, empathy mapping, HMW statements, and KPIs.

Design for spread and scale.

- Denise Gershbein @ Frog

NO.3

Ideate on possible solutions.

It’s important to ideate on as many solutions as possible. Keeping an open mind and exploring ideas will shine light on powerful concepts. I get as many concepts out as possible and vote on the most effective solutions.

Innovation is often a product of constraint.

- Todd Waterbury @ Target

NO.4

Prototype and improve ideas.

It’s important to test at every step of design and development. From paper prototypes to coded views, I validate my ideas to ensure I’m always on the right path and creating features that my users need and love.

Focus unswervingly on the customer.

- Jesse Hertzberg @ Squarespace

NO.5

Validate your findings.

Once features have been released into a product, it’s important to validate with data. Data is a tally of interactions that can inform decisions and validate KPIs. It’s vital I’m building something the user wants.

Netflix is unapologetically data driven.

- Rochelle King @ Netflix
